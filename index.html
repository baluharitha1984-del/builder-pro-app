The Psychology of Urge Surfing

1. Don't Suppress, Surf

Suppressing an urge only causes dynamic tension to grow. Urge Surfing teaches you to accept the feeling as a wave that crests, diminishes, and washes ashore without having to yield to it.

2. Replace with friction

Every bad habit must have a replacement habit that delivers similar stimulus but works productively. Set up physical friction so your bad habit takes at least 20 seconds to begin.

3. Non-Judgmental Reflection

Slipping up is rich psychological data. It reveals your triggers (Stress, fatigue, routines). Do not self-flagellate; log the trigger, adjust your replacement, and start again.
